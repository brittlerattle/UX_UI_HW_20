Salida Mountain Trails

Salida Mountain Trails is a great non-profit with a mission that was easy to get behind. But like many non-profits, SMT devotes most of their time and money to solving problems bigger than website usability— that’s where our team comes in.

My role in the redesign was mainly research and prototype design, though I played a role in each step at some level. Early on I used the cycling tracker Strava to target mountain bikers living in Salida to understand how local users feel about the organization and it’s trails. I also leveraged cyclist friends on Instagram for one on one user interviews.

When the time came to start prototyping, I rebuilt the logo using Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop and built and tested using Figma. The team used Google Fonts to find fonts that would be available to all that would fit the new aesthetic. Figjam was used to build an empathy map, leverage card sorting and formulate a site map

My key takeaways from the redesign were mainly how important stakeholder input and feedback is. Our stakeholder contact was out of the country when the project began, so we had to complete the redesign without ever knowing anything about site metrics or engagement levels within the non-profit.

It was also abundantly clear that a site can be iterated, improved upon and tested for months, so it was bittersweet to have to pass it off with such a quick turnaround.

Empathize

Before we could build a great non-profit site, the first step was to understand who we were designing for. We began our proto-persona from interacting with the original SMT site and gave it a demographic profile based on secondary research.
